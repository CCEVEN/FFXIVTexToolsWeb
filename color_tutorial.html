Adding more colors to a texture

The Alpha Channel in the Normal Map determines where the colors are placed in the texture


How it works:
The colors in the Alpha Channel of the Normal Map range from white to black and are divided into 16 sections, one for each of the 16 pixels of the color texture, black being the very top, and white at the bottom.

I will be using Bridesmaids Tights as an example.

Note: The extra row to the left is not part of the color texture and is put there as a reference.
Each of these shades of gray is associated with the colors in the row they are in.
So as an example, I chose to use the shade of gray I highlighted, so I've modified the colors directly to the right of it.

I then colored in blue for color, black for metallic, and blue again for glow.

The next step is to get that gray color associated with that section(highlighted in red), go over to the alpha channel in the Normal Map and color the part of the texture we want to use that new color in.

So here I've colored in a line in the leg part of the texture with the color associated with the row in the color texture.

Note: It can sometimes be difficult to determine which part is which, while at times it is very obvious. Experiment with different parts if you are unsure.
Save and Import the texture, making sure the settings for saving are correct.

This can be replicated with any piece of equipment, you have 16 segments that can all be different colors, metallic shine, and glow, which can then be painted onto any part of the texture. This makes it coloring of any texture highly customizable.
